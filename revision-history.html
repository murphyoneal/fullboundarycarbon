Site record

Revision history

A complete version log of every material change to the documents and pages on this site. The log is part of the evidentiary standard the site is built to — if something changes, it is recorded here, with a reason.

Why this page exists

The record is part of the method

Audit work depends on a clean record of what was asserted and when. This site treats its own publication history the same way it treats the public record it documents: as a dated, sourced, durable log. When a document is revised, the prior version's existence is recorded here rather than erased. When a number, a citation, or a framing changes, the change is noted. The purpose is twofold — to give readers a transparent account of how the work has developed, and to maintain the chain-of-notice record that the feedback channel feeds into.

Current document versions are linked from their own pages. This page is the consolidated index. Where a document carries its own internal version history, that history is reproduced in summary here; the document itself remains the primary record.

What changed, by release

Site v2.9 — June 2026

"Is GWP Solving Anything?" — published as working draft (Companion Paper No. 04). A new technical report opening with a methodological question — does GWP accounting verify climate outcomes, or merely enable material comparison — before quantifying the pre-extraction zero-baseline convention in GWP-based material accounting: identifies the specific ISO/CEN standards committees and dates responsible for the convention, traces a second, earlier instance of the same pattern in the IPCC's own 1996/2006 biogenic-carbon sector-boundary rule (which carried an explicit "not a conclusion about carbon neutrality" disclaimer that was not preserved in downstream construction standards), quantifies the carbon-flux omission using the Maxwell et al. (2019, Science Advances) forgone-removals method, and compares the resulting global-scale estimate against combined global steel and cement emissions. Published with an explicit on-page draft notice — not yet legally reviewed, not yet part of the cited DRL evidentiary record. The published draft incorporates corrections identified across four independent AI-engine technical/legal/scientific reviews: corrected forgone-removals arithmetic (the multiplier is applied to the net-sink-derived term, not to direct emissions directly), softened language describing standards-committee participation and ore comparisons to remove implied causation and overreach, a new section directly addressing the land-use-conversion counterfactual objection, a new section on GWP time-horizon sensitivity, and a nine-item limitations section.

Site v2.8 — June 2026

The Reagan Forestry Legacy and the American Housing Crisis — v3.4. Two new sections integrated: Section 6, "The Checkoff, the Standard, and the Procurement Instrument" (the Softwood Lumber Board commodity checkoff, the Buy Clean federal procurement instrument, and the carbon-neutrality underwriting layer connecting them); and Section 7, "The Competing Materials Context: Tariff Sequencing and Asymmetric Policy Infrastructure" (the Section 232 steel and aluminium tariff sequence, the Canada softwood lumber asymmetry, and what the sequencing shows). Existing Sections 6 through 12 renumbered to 8 through 14 throughout, including all subsection numbering. Document-wide language pass applied following external technical and legal review: "monopoly" framing replaced with "asymmetry" / "asymmetric apparatus" throughout (Section 4 title and subsections, Section 12, Section 14); "political permission structure" replaced with "policy configuration"; "single-material program" replaced with "single-material apparatus." A methodology paragraph was added to the opening of Section 4 disclosing the review's inclusion criteria, period (2012–2026), and source restrictions, and noting the method is conservative — it undercounts rather than overcounts the asymmetry it documents.

Materials Timeline — event-popup interaction rebuilt. Stacked events (years with two or more events landing on the same vertical line) are now rendered as individually offset, individually clickable lines rather than a single overlapping line. Clicking a chart line opens a popup panel with the full event description; clicking an event card in the grid below highlights and scrolls to the matching line on the chart and opens the same popup — bidirectional linking between the chart and the card list. Each chart line carries its own colour-coded category dot and short label. Site favicon added to the page (was previously missing from the browser tab).

Feedback form installed. The Tally submission form (previously listed as "being installed") is now embedded directly on the feedback page. Email remains available as an alternative contact method.

The Tree of Many Sales

v0.3 — May 2026 (working draft). Published as a working draft. Documents the over-counting half of the configuration: the same physical tonne of sequestered carbon counted as a benefit three to six times across the offset registry, national inventory, biogenic-neutral EPD, building certification, corporate Scope 3, and procurement systems, against an end-of-life methane debit allocated to none of them. Part I follows the carbon; Part II follows the asset (sixteen monetisation events in the US, twelve in NZ); Part III synthesises. Structural mechanisms anchored to statutory, regulatory, and program-record sources; typical-magnitude figures are working estimates pending a primary-source verification pass. Offered openly for review and disputation.
